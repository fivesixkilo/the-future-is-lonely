I should be afraid.

It’s ok though, I’m not alone.

I look at the others, they look like they know where they’re going.

And I hope I’ll know when I get there.

The train is full of people, all staring out into nothing.

We all should be afraid, but we’re together, and so it’s ok.

And then.

It always happens the same way.

Someone turns to me - their face isn’t like a person’s.

It’s twisted and mottled. I look into their eyes

I only see myself.

They’re furious. I’ve done something

Or maybe failed to do something

And they hate me, more than anything.

They scream. Sometimes words, sometimes just

Feelings, thoughts, ideas.

Slowly, everyone else joins in. Blank faces twisting into nothing but frothing mouths and gnashing teeth.

They tell me it’s my fault we’re on this train.

I should have known where it was going, why didn’t I warn them?

I’m sorry.

I want to say “I’m sorry,” but I can’t move my lips.

They tell me I’m going to die on this train.

I believe them.

They take my arms and legs and twist and pull like a child with a spider before forcing the pieces back together.

And then

With no warning

The train stops

The doors slide back

The sea of faces parts

I could walk out

The doors are open

I look outside, and I don’t see anything.

But surely, whatever is out there is better.

But I hesitate

I don’t get up

And I don’t walk towards the doors

I watch them close

And the press of people closes in around me

Why can’t I ever leave the train?
